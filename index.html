Hi, I’m Joonas, front-end dev from Turku, Finland. I don’t just code, I also do statistics / UI design / animation / photo- & cinematography / editing / economics / AB-testing / copywriting / back-end / archaeology*
*Well, I guess you won’t need that one
That's me, on the shores of Bremen, Germany, getting to know local Middle Ages.
I am an avid believer of continuous and fearless learning. For the past four years I have been doing my PhD in archaeology, studying medieval economics. I am a digital humanist and project-oriented team player, trying to see the people behind the data by using quantitative methods: statistics and social network analysis.
But I am also an artist with passion to create visually appealing designs, photos, stories and cinema. As an side-job entrepreneur, I have shot music videos, promos for social media use and designed layouts. I have published short stories, columns and even written on Turun Sanomat, Finlands third most read newspaper. I have also shared my expertise on TV-productions.
After discovering the passion to code (Java was my gateway drug), I decided that once I’m in the stage of finishing my PhD I want more coding. So finally, I enrolled to H2C program in Oulu university of Applied Sciences and now I am looking for a entry-level developer position.
First and foremost, I want to code. But if you see a benefit of having an in-house jack of all trades who is eager to learn more, you might want to consider if I should be the one as your next piece in the jigsaw puzzle.

Library-app

(In progress)
As universities made their library-services 'more efficient', departments were forced to gather even larger unofficial hand libraries. These libraries, which often originates from the 80's, have therefore become uncataloqued. To make matter worse, loaning systems are either antique paper-based or non-existent. Nobody knows who has borrowed what book and to whom to go to find a book you know exists.
The library app will be a tool to search, loan and request books in these cataloques. It will include an api to add new book, making the digitalization of the cataloque an crowd-source -project for the department. In addition, the department is given tools to track who can loan books, track borrowed books and gather statistics to justify their collection.

  • React
  • Back-end:
  • Node.js
  • MongoDB
